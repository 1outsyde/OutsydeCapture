Onboarding + Account Architecture Update

We need to restructure onboarding and account access rules to support three distinct signup paths, not two.

1️⃣ Onboarding must have THREE signup types (non-negotiable)

The Create Account / Onboarding screen must allow users to explicitly choose ONE of the following:

A) Consumer (Regular User)

Purpose: Browse, book, message, save, review

No dashboards

No business or photographer controls

Can apply as Influencer later

B) Business

Purpose: Sell products and/or services

Triggers Business Onboarding Flow

After onboarding:

Must select a subscription plan

Must complete Stripe subscription checkout

Must later complete Stripe Connect to receive payments

Gets Business Dashboard

C) Photographer

Purpose: Offer bookable photography services

Triggers Photographer Onboarding Flow

No subscription plan

Must complete Stripe Connect before receiving bookings

Gets Photographer Dashboard

⚠️ Do not merge Business + Photographer into one path.
They are separate signup choices from the very first screen.

2️⃣ Update the Create Account screen UI

Replace the current two-option layout with three selectable cards/buttons:

Consumer

Business

Photographer

The selected role must be:

Stored at signup

Sent to backend

Used for role-based access throughout the app

3️⃣ Account Screen — strict role-based visibility
Consumer / Guest

Account tab must show:

Edit Profile

Notifications

Saved Items

Apply as Influencer ✅

Must NOT show:

Become a Business or Photographer

Business Dashboard

Photographer Dashboard

Photographer

Account tab shows:

Photographer Dashboard

Edit Profile

Notifications

Earnings / Bookings (via dashboard)

Must NOT show:

Business Dashboard

Business onboarding options

Business

Account tab shows:

Business Dashboard

Edit Profile

Notifications

Earnings / Orders / Bookings (via dashboard)

Must NOT show:

Photographer Dashboard

Photographer onboarding options

4️⃣ Influencer is NOT a signup role

“Apply as Influencer” is available to Consumers & Guests

Influencer application:

Uses an open backend endpoint

Does NOT convert account role

Does NOT grant dashboard access

Influencer status is additive, not a primary role

5️⃣ Technical enforcement (important)

Role must come from backend (e.g. /api/me)

No UI-based assumptions

If role is missing or consumer → treat as consumer

Dashboards unlock only if role matches

6️⃣ Explicit exclusions

Dashboards are post-onboarding tools, not signup destinations

Users must land on Home/Search after signup, not dashboards

No “upgrade to business/photographer” shortcuts in Account
