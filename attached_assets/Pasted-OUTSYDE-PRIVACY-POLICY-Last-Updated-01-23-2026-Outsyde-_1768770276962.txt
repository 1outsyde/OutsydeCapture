OUTSYDE COOKIES NOTICE

Last Updated: 01/23/2026

This Cookies Notice explains how Outsyde (“Outsyde,” “we,” “our,” or “us”) uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit or interact with our website, mobile application, and related services (collectively, the “Platform”).

By continuing to use Outsyde, you agree to our use of cookies as described in this notice.

1. WHAT ARE COOKIES?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website or use an application. They help platforms remember information about your visit and improve user experience.

Cookies may be:

Session cookies (deleted when you close your browser)

Persistent cookies (remain until deleted or expired)

2. TYPES OF COOKIES WE USE
A. Essential Cookies

These cookies are required for the Platform to function properly, including:

Account authentication

Security and fraud prevention

Session management

Without these cookies, core features may not work.

B. Performance & Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how users interact with Outsyde by collecting information such as:

Pages visited

Feature usage

Error reports

Performance metrics

This data is used to improve the Platform’s functionality and experience.

C. Functionality Cookies

These cookies allow Outsyde to:

Remember user preferences

Save login sessions

Personalize content and settings

D. Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services (such as analytics or payment providers) that place cookies on your device.

These third parties are governed by their own privacy and cookie policies. Outsyde does not control their use of cookies.

3. COOKIES IN MOBILE APPLICATIONS

While cookies are primarily associated with web browsers, mobile applications may use similar technologies (such as SDKs or device identifiers) to:

Maintain sessions

Track performance

Improve stability and security

4. HOW TO MANAGE COOKIES

You may control or delete cookies through your browser or device settings.

Please note:

Disabling cookies may impact Platform functionality

Certain features may not work properly without cookies

5. DO NOT TRACK SIGNALS

Outsyde does not currently respond to browser “Do Not Track” signals, as there is no consistent industry standard for compliance.

6. CHANGES TO THIS COOKIES NOTICE

Outsyde may update this Cookies Notice at any time. Changes become effective when posted. Continued use of the Platform constitutes acceptance of the updated notice.
